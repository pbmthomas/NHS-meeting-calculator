NHS Meeting Cost Calculator

2025/26 pay scales · England · AfC / Consultant / VSM

This tool calculates how much an NHS meeting costs based on who attends and how long it lasts. It uses the official 2025/26 pay scales for England. You don't need to install anything — it runs entirely in your web browser. No data is sent anywhere; everything stays on your device.

  1. Choose your mode (the two tabs below these instructions)
    Pick "Live meeting" if you want to run a timer during a meeting that's happening right now. Pick "Past meeting" if the meeting has already happened and you want to type in how long it lasted.
  2. Add each person who attended
    Scroll down to the grey "Add attendee" box. For each person in the meeting:

    1. Pick their pay framework: most NHS staff are "Agenda for Change" (nurses, AHPs, admin, managers). Hospital doctors in consultant posts → "Consultant". Trust board members (CEO, CFO, COO, etc.) → "Very Senior Manager".

    2. Pick their band or threshold. If you're not sure of the exact one, a rough guess is fine — the salary shown next to each option will help you judge.

    3. Set the uplift %. Leave this at 0 for most people. Use 20 for consultants doing extra programmed activities, or any other local supplement.

    4. Click the blue "+ Add" button.

    Repeat for every person. You'll see them listed below. Click the × next to anyone to remove them.
  3. If using "Live meeting" mode — press the blue Start button when the meeting begins
    The cost ticks up in real time like a taxi meter. Press Pause to stop, Resume to continue, and Reset to clear everything. You can share your screen during a Teams call to show the running total.
  4. If using "Past meeting" mode — type in how long the meeting lasted
    Enter hours and minutes. For example, a 90-minute meeting = 1 hour and 30 minutes. The cost updates instantly as you type.
  5. Read the summary at the bottom
    This shows the cost per minute, the total for the meeting, and — if this meeting happens regularly — what it costs over a month and a full year. The "fully loaded" figure (with on-costs ticked) is the true cost to the trust.

Jargon buster

Agenda for Change (AfC)The NHS pay system covering most staff — nurses, AHPs, admin, managers, etc. Has 9 pay bands.
Band (e.g. Band 7)A pay grade within AfC. Higher band = more senior role and higher salary.
VSMVery Senior Manager — trust board-level roles like CEO, CFO, COO, Medical Director.
Threshold (1–5)Consultant pay is in thresholds based on years of experience as a consultant, not AfC bands.
Uplift %An extra percentage added on top of base salary — e.g. 20% for consultants doing additional PAs.
On-costs (≈35%)What the trust pays on top of salary: employer's National Insurance (~15%), NHS pension contribution (~20.6%), and apprenticeship levy (~0.5%). This is the real cost to the organisation.
£/hr (hourly rate)Annual salary ÷ 1,957.5 (the contracted hours in a full-time NHS year: 37.5 hrs/week × 52.14 weeks).
